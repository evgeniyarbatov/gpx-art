# Questions

*A line drawn by the body through space. Two coordinates at a time. Nothing more — and everything.*

These are not problems to solve. They are doors to walk through. The answer is not in the file, not in the render, not in the reply — it is in the next walk, the next glance, the next breath.

---

## On the line itself

- If you remove every coordinate except the first and the last, what remains of the walk?
- When `skeleton` finds the turning bones of a route, is it revealing structure or inventing it?
- Does a line that never closes still want to become a circle?
- If two people walk the same path on the same day, in opposite directions, do they draw the same artwork?
- At what zoom level does a run stop being a run and start being handwriting?
- Is a GPS trace the walk, a memory of the walk, or a third thing that watched from above?
- If the satellite blinked for thirty seconds and the gap were filled with gold (`kintsugi`), would the walk be more true or less?
- Can a straight line be as honest as a wandering one — or is honesty only found in the detour?

---

## On the body as instrument

- Who is holding the brush — the legs, the lungs, the will, or the habit that turned left instead of right?
- When pace becomes line weight, does slowing down make you more visible or merely thicker?
- If heart rate were audible in the image, would you walk differently to compose the sound?
- Is fatigue a color you have not yet named?
- Does the body know it is making art while it moves, or only afterward, when the PNG appears?
- What is the difference between a run undertaken for fitness and a run undertaken for an image that does not yet exist?
- If you walked a kanji (`shodō`) and no one could read it at map scale, was it still calligraphy?

---

## On seeing and style

- Which style tells the truth of a walk — and can a walk have more than one truth?
- When `painting` scatters ink along the path, is it depicting the route or the weather inside you that day?
- If `decay` dissolves the trace, what is left — the walk, or the fact that something was once there?
- Does `rain` fall on the path or through it?
- When `contour` stacks parallel lines, is it drawing topography, fabric, or the many possible versions of a single choice?
- If every style lies a little, which lie do you prefer — and why?
- Can a style you wrote in Python ever surprise you, the way your own footstep sometimes does?
- What would `whisper` — the faintest possible line — refuse to say?

---

## On emptiness (間 *ma*)

- If seventy percent of the canvas is empty, who owns the white — the walker or the viewer?
- Is negative space silence, or is it the walk continuing where you did not go?
- When you center the track, what are you pushing to the margins — landscape, fear, or the parts of the day that had no coordinates?
- Does a line placed low on the page feel heavier than one that rises?
- If you deleted the line entirely and kept only the margins, would the artwork be complete?
- How much emptiness can a single stroke hold before it stops being a stroke and becomes a horizon?

---

## On time

- A GPX is a film strip with one frame showing — which frame did you choose by standing still?
- If the line drew itself point by point at real timestamps, would the pauses feel like rest or like doubt?
- If it drew at uniform speed, would the same walk become a different life?
- Can `decay` applied year after year to the same loop show wabi-sabi — or only erosion?
- Is the timestamp metadata, or is it the pulse of the work?
- What season is encoded in a track that never records temperature?

---

## On intention and accident

- When you design a route before walking (`ensō`, a horizon line, a character), who leads — the sketch or the sidewalk that refuses it?
- If the circle does not close, is the gap wabi-sabi or failure?
- Are GPS dropouts errors, or the walk insisting on its own punctuation?
- When DTW selects diverse tracks, is it curating difference — or protecting you from seeing the same thing twice?
- Does a wrong turn become composition, or does composition require the wrong turn?
- If you walked for the image alone and the image disappoints, was the walk wasted?

---

## On place and displacement

- The line floats in a white void — is it everywhere, or nowhere, or more honest that way?
- If you laid a faint coastline beneath the track, would you be grounding the walk or borrowing a place it did not earn?
- Can the same shape walked in two cities be one artwork or two confessions?
- Does a trail know it has been rendered?
- What is the relationship between the miles in your legs and the inches on the wall?
- If someone else runs your route in a second color, do you share a line or merely overlap?

---

## On the recipe and the mystery

- The QR code links to the Python that made the image — does knowing the recipe deepen the meal or spoil it?
- If the gist hash never changes but the walk never repeats, which is the original — the code or the footstep?
- Is the artwork the PNG, the function, the GPX, or the afternoon?
- When a stranger scans the code, what are they looking for — instructions, or proof that a body was here?
- Can code be a form of poetry if nobody runs it?
- If you open the style registry to others, does authorship travel with the `@style` decorator or with the feet that earned the track?

---

## On collection and narrative

- Twenty diverse tracks selected by algorithm — is that an exhibition or a crowd?
- What order should walks be hung in: chronological, geographical, emotional, or by how much they hurt?
- If each image had a haiku (`haiga`), would the text explain the line or compete with it?
- When does a folder of PNGs become a book, and when does a book become a practice?
- Is there a number of walks after which the collection starts walking you?

---

## On material and scale

- If a plotter re-walks your walk with a pen on paper, which passage is the original — flesh or machine?
- Does `stitch` want to become thread on cloth, and if it does, is the embroidery the walk or a copy of a copy?
- At what size does a line stop being a map and start being a landscape you can enter?
- A small card in the pocket versus a large print on the wall — same track, same truth?
- When the work leaves the screen, what happens to the white void?

---

## On the horizon of this project

- You carve only the shadow (latitude, longitude) — what is the stone that casts it?
- What question would a style need to ask before you could write it?
- If restriction is the medium, what happens when you add elevation, heart rate, weather — do you gain a voice or lose the koan?
- Is the next phase of the project technical, or is it a longer pause at the bench?
- What would make you stop generating images and start sitting with one?
- How do you know when a style is finished — when the code works, or when you no longer feel the need to walk that way?
- What is the question this repository is afraid to ask?
- What would you walk this week if you believed the image already existed somewhere in the line, waiting?

---

## One question to carry

> Before you leave the house: are you going for a run, or is the run coming for you?

Walk. Render if you must. Hang two versions side by side. Write nothing in the margin, or write everything. The roadmap begins with your feet.

*「行雲流水」 — like clouds moving, like water flowing. The line does not insist.*
